\begin{abstract}
Routing LLM queries to the right model for each query is critical for balancing cost and quality in production systems.
We present RouteSmith, a multi-strategy LLM routing system. This paper focuses on its online bandit component, which uses Linear Thompson Sampling (LinTS) over a 27-dimensional query feature vector to balance cost and quality without pretraining labels, hyperparameter tuning, or architectural limits on the number of model arms ($K \geq 2$). The full product additionally includes a random forest quality predictor for deployment scenarios where offline calibration data is available.

On MMLU (600 questions, GPT-4o vs GPT-4o-mini), LinTS-27d achieves 75.8\% accuracy at \$0.116 cost --- a 46\% cost reduction over Static-Strong (\$0.214) while maintaining 59.3\% of the performance gap recovery (APGR).
LinUCB-27d achieves APGR=1.126, exceeding Static-Strong accuracy (78.2\% vs 77.7\%) by routing selectively.
In a domain-transfer evaluation, RouteLLM-SW routes 0\% of queries to the strong model on these structured benchmarks\mbox{---}a known limitation when Chatbot Arena conversational embeddings are applied to MMLU/GSM8K question answering (evaluated with \texttt{all-MiniLM-L6-v2} fallback embeddings rather than the original \texttt{text-embedding-3-small}).

In a 5-arm multi-model experiment (GPT-4o, Claude-Sonnet-4.5, Qwen-Plus, MiniMax-M1, DeepSeek-V3), LinTS-27d achieves 71.0\%$\pm$0.3\% accuracy at \$0.117 cost --- 45\% cheaper than always-GPT-4o --- while discovering a stable routing policy that concentrates on GPT-4o and Qwen-Plus.
RouteSmith's $K$-arm bandit formulation naturally extends to $K > 2$ arms, a structural advantage over binary routers.
\end{abstract}
