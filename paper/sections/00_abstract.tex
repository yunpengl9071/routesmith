\begin{abstract}
Routing LLM queries to the right model for each query is critical for balancing cost and quality in production systems.
We present RouteSmith, a contextual bandit router that learns online from per-query feedback using a 27-dimensional feature vector encoding query complexity, domain, and structure.
RouteSmith requires zero pretraining labels, supports $K \geq 2$ model arms, and uses Linear Thompson Sampling (LinTS-27d) for parameter-free exploration without a $\beta$ hyperparameter.

On MMLU (600 questions, GPT-4o vs GPT-4o-mini), LinTS-27d achieves 75.8\% accuracy at \$0.116 cost --- a 46\% cost reduction over Static-Strong (\$0.214) while maintaining 59.3\% of the performance gap recovery (APGR).
LinUCB-27d achieves APGR=1.126, exceeding Static-Strong accuracy (78.2\% vs 77.7\%) by routing selectively.
In contrast, RouteLLM-SW achieves APGR=$-$0.111 on MMLU across all thresholds, routing 0\% of queries to the strong model due to domain mismatch between Chatbot Arena embeddings and structured benchmarks.

In a 5-arm multi-model experiment (GPT-4o, Claude-Sonnet-4.5, Qwen-Plus, MiniMax-M1, DeepSeek-V3), LinTS-27d achieves 71.0\%$\pm$0.3\% accuracy at \$0.117 cost --- 45\% cheaper than always-GPT-4o --- while discovering a stable routing policy that concentrates on GPT-4o and Qwen-Plus.
RouteLLM-SW cannot extend to $K > 2$ arms without $O(K^2)$ re-training, a structural limitation RouteSmith avoids by design.
\end{abstract}
